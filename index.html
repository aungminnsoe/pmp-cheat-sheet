6 Non-Negotiable Exam Rules
1
First Instinct
Only change if you have a SPECIFIC, CONCRETE reason — not a feeling. "I misread X" or "I confused X with Y" = valid. Vague doubt = keep your answer.
2
Complete Solution
Does my answer solve ALL problems — including hidden ones? PMI always embeds a secondary problem. Find the answer that solves both.
3
Assess First
Gather full info before acting — UNLESS there is an imminent irreversible consequence (safety, live breach, knowledge walking out door).
4
Authority Limits
Nobody overrides ethics, safety, or legal. Not sponsor. Not senior leadership. Not cultural norms. Not schedule pressure.
5
Systemic Beats Reactive
When problem is recurring ("third time", "consistently", "pattern"), choose the structural fix over the one-time fix.
6
Double Trap
Don't apply the last question's lesson mechanically to the next one. Each question is fresh context. PMI uses your own learning against you.
Master Decision Framework
1
Imminent irreversible harm?
YES → Act immediately. NO → Continue to Q2.
2
Ethics / Safety / Legal issue?
YES → Ethics wins. Always. No authority overrides it. NO → Continue to Q3.
3
Timeline critically tight already?
YES → Convene right people urgently. NO → Assess independently first.
4
Problem happened repeatedly?
YES → Systemic/structural fix. NO → Immediate resolution.
5
Assessed the situation yet?
NO → Assess first. YES → Choose appropriate action.
6
Answer solves ALL problems?
Check for hidden stakeholder, sponsor, or process element. If not — find the answer that does.

🎯 A.R.T Framework — Core Decision Sequence

For every scenario question, work through this sequence before picking an answer:

A
Assess / Analyze
Identify the root cause first. Collect data, evaluate impact on scope/schedule/budget/quality. Do NOT skip to action.
R
Review
Review the relevant plan (project management plan, risk register, resource management plan, stakeholder engagement plan, etc.).
T
Take Action
Only after A and R — meet with stakeholders, submit change request, implement response, escalate if needed.
⚠️ Exception: Skip A→R if there is imminent irreversible harm (safety breach, key knowledge walking out the door, health hazard). Act immediately.

EV Always Comes First
CPI = EV/AC  |  SPI = EV/PV
Never flip the numerator and denominator
Core Variables
PVPlanned Value — budgeted cost of work SCHEDULED (what you planned to spend by now)
EVEarned Value — budgeted cost of work PERFORMED (value of work actually completed)
ACActual Cost — actual cost of work PERFORMED (what you actually spent)
BACBudget At Completion — total approved budget for the entire project
Performance Indices
CPI — Cost Performance Index
EV / AC
>1.0 = under budget ✅ | <1.0 = over budget ❌ | =1.0 = on budget
SPI — Schedule Performance Index
EV / PV
>1.0 = ahead of schedule ✅ | <1.0 = behind ❌ | =1.0 = on schedule
CV — Cost Variance
EV - AC
>0 = under budget | <0 = over budget
SV — Schedule Variance
EV - PV
>0 = ahead of schedule | <0 = behind schedule
Forecasting Formulas
EAC — at current efficiency rate
BAC / CPI
If current CPI continues for rest of project. Trigger: "based on current performance"
EAC — at budgeted rate (CPI=1.0)
AC + (BAC - EV)
Remaining work done at planned rate. Trigger: "budgeted rate" or "planned rate"
ETC — at current efficiency rate
(BAC - EV) / CPI
Cost to complete remaining work at CURRENT efficiency
ETC — at budgeted rate
BAC - EV
Cost to complete at planned rate (divide by 1.0). Trigger: "budgeted rate"
VAC — Variance At Completion
BAC - EAC
>0 = under budget at completion | <0 = over budget at completion
TCPI — To Complete Performance Index
(BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC)
Efficiency needed to finish within original budget. >1.0 = must work harder than current pace
Key trigger words: "current rate/efficiency" → use CPI | "budgeted/planned rate" → use 1.0 (BAC-EV)

Risk Management Process
Plan → Identify → Qualitative → Quantitative → Plan Responses → Implement → Monitor
Risk = uncertain event with POSITIVE (opportunity) OR NEGATIVE (threat) impact. Both are risks!
Threat Responses — ATMA
Avoid ⭐Eliminate threat — change plan to remove risk entirely. Best for severe threats.
TransferShift impact to third party — insurance, fixed-price contracts. Risk still exists.
MitigateReduce probability or impact — most common. Add testing, redundancy, training.
AcceptActive = contingency plan ready. Passive = no action. Low priority risks only.
EscalateRisk outside project scope — escalate to program or portfolio level.
Opportunity Responses — ESEA
Exploit ⭐Ensure opportunity occurs — assign best resources, eliminate uncertainty
SharePartner with another party to capture opportunity together
EnhanceIncrease probability or positive impact of opportunity
AcceptTake advantage if it occurs — no proactive action needed

Conflict Resolution Sequence
Key question: "WHERE in this sequence are we right now?" The scenario always tells you.
1
No attempt yet → Understand separately
Private individual conversations first. Understand each party's underlying interests.
2
Spoken separately → Facilitated joint meeting
Surface root cause together. Collaborative problem-solving with both parties present.
3
Joint meeting failed → Escalate
Escalate to appropriate authority. Not before — never skip steps 1 and 2.
4
Escalation failed → Formal intervention
HR, legal, restructure, reassignment. True last resort only.
Conflict Resolution Methods — Best to Worst
Collaborate ⭐Win-win. Address root cause. Mutually acceptable solution. PMI PREFERRED.
CompromiseBoth parties give something. Partially satisfies both. Second choice.
AccommodateEmphasize agreement, downplay differences. Temporary — does not resolve underlying conflict.
AvoidPostpone or retreat. Never resolves conflict. Cooling-down period only.
Force ⛔ WORSTWin-lose. Impose your view. Damages relationships. PMI LEAST preferred.

Control chartProcess over time. UCL/LCL limits. Rule of 7 = seven consecutive points same side = out of control.
Scatter diagramCorrelation between two variables. Does not prove causation.
HistogramDistribution of data — frequency of occurrences across ranges
Decision treeEMV analysis. Multiply probability by impact across all branches. Sum for expected outcome.
Monte CarloSimulation using ranges — probability of completing within budget or schedule
Tornado diagramSensitivity analysis — which risks have most impact. Widest bar = highest sensitivity.
AnalogousBased on similar past project. Top-down. Less accurate. Faster. Early in project when little detail known.
ParametricStatistical — cost per unit multiplied by number of units. More accurate than analogous.
Bottom-up ⭐Estimate each work package and roll up. Most accurate. Most time-consuming.
PERT(O + 4M + P) / 6. Accounts for uncertainty. SD = (P-O)/6.
Planning pokerAgile — team consensus story point estimation. Reduces anchoring bias.
T-shirt sizingAgile relative sizing (XS/S/M/L/XL) for quick initial rough estimation
